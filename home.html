Our Mission

Why SDG 7 Matters Today

Energy poverty is not a distant issue — it affects communities everywhere. As students and citizens, we have real power to accelerate clean energy adoption.

Access for All

Universal Energy Access

Over 700 million people still lack access to electricity. Affordable solar solutions can transform lives — powering schools, clinics, and businesses in remote areas.

  • Advocate for community solar micro-grids in your area
  • Support energy-access charities with donations or volunteering
  • Share reliable information about energy poverty online
Student Action

What Students Can Do

You don't need a solar farm to make a difference. Small, consistent actions create measurable impact — especially when adopted collectively across campuses.

  • Switch to a renewable-energy tariff at home
  • Join or start an energy-saving society at university
  • Reduce device charging waste with smart power strips
Outcomes

The World We Build Together

If widespread clean energy adoption were achieved, global CO₂ emissions could fall by 70%, energy costs could halve, and millions could escape poverty.

  • 70% reduction in energy-related CO₂ emissions
  • Millions lifted out of energy poverty by 2030
  • Cleaner air improving public health in cities globally
